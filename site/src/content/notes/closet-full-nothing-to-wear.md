The most common thing I hear standing in a client's closet: *"I have so
much stuff, and nothing to wear."* Both halves are true, and the
second one has almost nothing to do with the first.

"Nothing to wear" is rarely a clothes problem. It's a **decisions
problem**. Every piece in your closet that doesn't fit, doesn't suit
your life anymore, or carries a little guilt ("it was expensive," "it
might come back") is a decision you have to re-make every single
morning. Thirty unmade decisions between you and your coffee.

That's why the fix isn't shopping. It's editing:

- **Fit today.** If it doesn't fit the body you have this season, it's
  not a choice; it's a critic. Out of the daily rotation it goes.
- **Life today.** Your wardrobe should serve the life on your calendar,
  not the one from five years ago.
- **Feeling today.** Keep what makes you stand differently. "Fine" is
  not a feeling worth hanger space.

What's left after a real edit is smaller and works harder, because
everything in view is a *yes*. The mornings get quiet. The full-closet
feeling gets replaced by the much better one: *I know what I'm wearing.*
