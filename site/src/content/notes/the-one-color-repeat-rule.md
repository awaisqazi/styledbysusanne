Here's the styling principle I use more than any other, and it takes
ten seconds: **pick one color in your outfit and let it appear exactly
twice.**

A blush shoe that repeats the blush in your scarf. A white knit that
repeats the white in a printed skirt. Gold jewelry that repeats a gold
belt buckle. That's it. The repetition tells the eye *this was decided,
not defaulted*, which is what "put together" actually means.

Three ways to use it tomorrow morning:

1. **Print first.** Wearing a pattern? Pull its quietest color and
   repeat it in your top, shoe, or bag.
2. **Accessory echo.** Two accessories in the same tone (scarf and
   shoe, earring and belt) count as a repeat.
3. **Tonal cheat.** Head-to-toe neutrals? Repeat a *texture* instead:
   two ribbed pieces, two satin pieces.

The repeat is doing the work a stylist does: it creates a reason the
pieces are together. Once you see it, you can't unsee it, and getting
dressed gets faster.
